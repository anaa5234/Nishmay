Kashi- The Mayanagri of Gods

Varanasi, also called Banaras or Kashi, is one of the oldest inhabited cities in the world from the bustling markets to the serene ghats, Varanasi offers a unique blend of ancient and modern India.

 

This ancient city in Uttar Pradesh holds immense cultural and religious significance, known for its vibrant festivals and rituals, which are deeply rooted in Hindu mythology and tradition. Being the hub of spirituality and artistry, Varanasi has bred master artisans in every corner of the city.

Nepali Mandir

Located on the Lalita Ghat of Varanasi, this temple holds significant religious and cultural importance as the temple is dedicated to the Hindu deity, Mahadev.

 

The whole temple is built in Nepali style architecture with a pagoda style building made of Terracotta, stone and termite resistant wood making it one of the oldest and most famous temples of Varanasi. The entirety of the temple is adorned with beautiful wood carved panels depicting apsaras and faces.
